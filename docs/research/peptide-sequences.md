# What is a Peptide Sequence

A peptide sequence refers to the specific order of amino acids linked together by peptide bonds to form a peptide molecule.

The sequence of amino acids determines the peptide's structural and chemical characteristics.

Even small changes in sequence may significantly influence peptide behavior in laboratory experiments.

---

# Amino Acids and Peptide Bonds

Peptides are composed of amino acids connected through **peptide bonds**.

Each amino acid contributes unique properties that influence the peptide's overall characteristics.

Common amino acids found in peptides include:

• glycine (G)  
• alanine (A)  
• lysine (K)  
• arginine (R)  
• serine (S)  
• tyrosine (Y)

The arrangement of these amino acids forms the peptide sequence.

---

# Peptide Length

Peptides may vary in length depending on the number of amino acids present.

General classifications include:

• short peptides (2–10 amino acids)  
• oligopeptides (10–20 amino acids)  
• polypeptides (20+ amino acids)

Peptide length can influence molecular weight, solubility, and experimental behavior.

---

# Sequence Representation

Researchers typically represent peptide sequences using **single-letter amino acid codes**.

For example:

Gly-Lys-Ser-Tyr

may be represented as:

G-K-S-Y

This notation allows scientists to quickly reference peptide composition.
