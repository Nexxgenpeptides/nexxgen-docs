# What Are Lyophilized Peptides

Lyophilized peptides are peptides that have undergone a freeze-drying process known as **lyophilization**.

Lyophilization removes water from the peptide solution under controlled temperature and pressure conditions, producing a dry, stable powder.

This process helps preserve peptide integrity during storage and transportation.

---

# Why Peptides Are Lyophilized

Freeze-drying peptides provides several advantages for laboratory research materials:

• improved long-term stability  
• reduced risk of hydrolysis or degradation  
• easier transportation and storage  
• extended shelf life when stored properly  

Because peptides can degrade in aqueous environments, removing water helps maintain structural stability.

---

# Lyophilization Process Overview

The lyophilization process typically involves three stages:

### Freezing
The peptide solution is rapidly frozen to preserve molecular structure.

### Primary Drying
Water is removed through sublimation under reduced pressure.

### Secondary Drying
Remaining moisture is removed to create a stable dry powder.

The result is a **lyophilized peptide cake or powder** that can later be reconstituted with an appropriate laboratory solvent.

---

# Storage of Lyophilized Peptides

Lyophilized peptides are typically stored under controlled laboratory conditions such as:

Short-term storage:  
2°C – 8°C (refrigeration)

Long-term storage:  
-20°C or lower

Protecting peptides from moisture, heat, and light may help maintain stability.
