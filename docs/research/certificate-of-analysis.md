# What is a Certificate of Analysis

A Certificate of Analysis (COA) is a document that provides analytical data verifying the identity, purity, and quality of a peptide sample.

COAs allow researchers to review laboratory testing results associated with a specific production lot.

This documentation supports transparency and traceability in laboratory research materials.

---

# Information Typically Included in a COA

A peptide Certificate of Analysis may include information such as:

• peptide name  
• batch or lot number  
• molecular formula  
• molecular weight  
• purity analysis results  
• analytical testing methods  
• date of analysis  

These details allow researchers to confirm the characteristics of the material being studied.

---

# Analytical Methods

Peptide COAs commonly reference analytical techniques including:

### High Performance Liquid Chromatography (HPLC)

HPLC analysis is used to evaluate peptide purity by separating compounds within a sample and measuring their relative abundance.

### Mass Spectrometry (MS)

Mass spectrometry is used to verify the molecular mass of the peptide and confirm its identity.

These analytical methods help ensure consistency and reproducibility in laboratory research.

# Lot Traceability

Each peptide batch is typically assigned a unique lot number.

Lot traceability allows researchers to reference the analytical documentation associated with a specific sample used in an experiment.
