# Factors Affecting Peptide Stability
Major stability factors include:
- temperature
- pH
- moisture
- oxidation
- light exposure
- repeated freeze-thaw cycles

---

# Temperature Effects
Higher temperatures may accelerate degradation through oxidation and hydrolysis.  
Most research materials are stored refrigerated or frozen.

---

# Moisture Sensitivity
Lyophilized peptides are generally stable when kept dry.  
Humidity exposure may cause degradation and aggregation.

---

# Oxidation
Certain residues may be more susceptible to oxidation, including:
- methionine
- cysteine
- tryptophan

Minimize air exposure where possible.

---

# Freeze–Thaw Cycles
Repeated freeze–thaw cycles may increase aggregation and reduce reproducibility.  
Researchers often use **single-use aliquots**.

---

# Light Sensitivity
Some peptides may degrade with prolonged light exposure.  
Store in dark or opaque containers where appropriate.
